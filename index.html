📜 The Brief History of Flash

Flash is a multimedia software platform used for creating animations, browser games, internet applications, desktop applications and mobile applications. Flash began its journey in 1996 and quickly became the dominant platform for interactive content on the web thanks to its low consumption of internet usage by vector graphics.

At its peak in the early 2000s, it powered over 98% of all desktop web browsers. It gave birth to many communities all around the world (e.g. Newgrounds forum, ShanKe, Flash-jp). Creators in the communities have more accessibility than ever to create millions of content – from viral animations like “The end of the World” to iconic web games like QWOP. Flash was the canvas to a generation of digital artists, animators, indie game developers and website hosts that they can put their talent in.

However, Flash has significant drawbacks, it was proprietary, resource- intensive, vulnerable in security and performed badly on mobile devices. With the rise of open web standards like HTML5, CSS3 and WebGL, the rise of smart phones and the boycott from tech companies, for instance: Steve Jobs’ famous open letter “Thoughts on Flash” (2010), the platform tsarted to decline. This decline continues till December 31, 2020 when Adobe officially ended support for Flash Player.

It is not a perfect tool, but a great contribution to the development of modern internet and web design.

1993 FutureWave Software founded; begins work on SmartSketch (a vector drawing app for pen computers).
1996 FutureSplash Animator released — the first version of what would become Flash.
1996 Macromedia acquires FutureWave and renames the product Macromedia Flash 1.0.
2000 Flash 5 introduces ActionScript, a full scripting language for interactive content.
2005 Adobe acquires Macromedia for $3.4 billion; Flash becomes Adobe Flash.
2007 YouTube (Flash-based) dominates online video; Flash reaches ~99% browser penetration.
2010 Steve Jobs publishes "Thoughts on Flash" — a major turning point against Flash on mobile.
2017 Adobe announces the end-of-life of Flash Player, set for 2020.
2020 December 31, 2020 — Adobe officially kills Flash Player; the era ends.
